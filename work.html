Digital Learning Platform User Experience Study

Multi-site Ethnographic Research | King’s College London

The Problem

A large-scale education initiative introduced digital learning platforms into public schools, but adoption and classroom integration were inconsistent. Stakeholders needed to understand how implementation differed across real classroom settings and why intended use often broke down in practice.

My Role & Methods

  • Led end-to-end qualitative research design and execution across multiple field sites
  • Conducted 30+ stakeholder interviews with teachers, administrators, and intermediary organizations
  • Collected and analyzed 200+ hours of classroom video and observational data
  • Mapped teacher workflows and classroom coordination patterns around shared devices and projected screens
  • Used NVivo/MAXQDA for systematic coding and synthesis
  • Translated findings into design and implementation recommendations for program stakeholders

Key Deliverables

  • Teacher workflow and classroom interaction maps
  • Friction point analysis for platform use in constrained classroom settings
  • Stakeholder-facing research summaries and implementation recommendations
  • Research presentations for academic and practitioner audiences

Impact

The research showed that “adoption” was not just a training issue. Teachers were actively adapting the platform to classroom realities, often repurposing features to fit time, infrastructure, and coordination constraints. These insights reframed the problem from user compliance to workflow fit, shaping more grounded design and support priorities.

Virtual Rituals and Platform Experience Research

NSF-Funded Project | George Washington University

The Problem

During COVID-19, communities moved important social and ritual events to video platforms. The team needed to understand how people adapted embodied, in-person practices to mediated environments, and what this revealed about interaction design, participation, and social presence.

My Role & Methods

  • Led qualitative research strategy for a multi-method study of virtual interaction
  • Conducted interviews and focus groups with participants and organizers
  • Analyzed recorded virtual events to identify recurring interaction patterns and coordination practices
  • Built structured comparisons across event types and user roles
  • Contributed to public-facing research storytelling and digital research outputs

Key Deliverables

  • User behavior and participation pattern analysis
  • Interaction findings on mediated presence and coordination
  • Research synthesis for mixed stakeholder audiences
  • Public-facing project communication materials

Impact

The study generated practical insight into how people preserve meaning and social coordination in digital environments. Findings helped frame better questions for platform design, especially around visibility, turn-taking, and ambient awareness.

Civic Engagement and Participation Process Study

Community Development Research | University of Massachusetts

The Problem

A community planning initiative aimed to increase public participation in local redevelopment, but faced uneven engagement and unclear communication between stakeholders. The project needed evidence on where participation broke down and how the process could be redesigned.

My Role & Methods

  • Led qualitative and mixed-methods field research on participation workflows
  • Conducted interviews, focus groups, and participant observation across community settings
  • Analyzed organizational communication and stakeholder narratives
  • Synthesized findings into practical recommendations for process redesign

Key Deliverables

  • Stakeholder maps and participation pathway analysis
  • Barrier and friction point synthesis
  • Action-oriented recommendations for community engagement design
  • Academic and applied research outputs

Impact

The research identified how communication structure and stakeholder role ambiguity limited participation. Findings informed changes to outreach and meeting design, and contributed to award-winning scholarly work on inequality and public participation.
