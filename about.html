About

I bring over seven years of applied qualitative research experience across academic, nonprofit, and product-facing environments. My PhD in Sociology (with a focus on ethnomethodology and conversation analysis) trained me to examine how people actually coordinate work, communication, and technology in everyday settings.

My research often focuses on high-complexity environments where digital tools, organizational constraints, and human interaction overlap. I am especially interested in workflows, collaboration, implementation gaps, and the practical work people do to make systems function.

Methodologically, I combine ethnography, interviews, video analysis, and qualitative synthesis. I do not just document what users say. I analyze how tasks unfold in practice, where friction happens, and what that means for product design, service design, or organizational strategy.

I have led multi-site qualitative studies, conducted hundreds of hours of field observation, built research narratives for mixed audiences, and translated dense findings into clear design and policy recommendations. I am currently focused on UX Research roles where I can apply ethnographic depth to product and service challenges.

Core Competencies: Qualitative research design, ethnography, stakeholder interviews, usability testing, video/interaction analysis, journey mapping, workshop facilitation, mixed methods, participatory design, research synthesis, insight storytelling.

Tools: NVivo, MAXQDA, Dedoose, Figma, Dscout, Mural, Miro, Tableau, GitHub, Remesh.

Location: Washington, D.C. (open to remote and hybrid roles)
